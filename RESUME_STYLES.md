# Resume Styles Guide

## Available Themes

### 1. Classic

- **Best for**: Traditional industries, conservative roles
- **Colors**: Navy blue headers, clean white background
- **Typography**: Professional serif/sans-serif mix
- **Layout**: Traditional chronological format

### 2. Professional

- **Best for**: Corporate positions, business roles
- **Colors**: Deep blue accents, subtle gray backgrounds
- **Typography**: Clean, readable fonts
- **Layout**: Structured sections with clear hierarchy

### 3. Modern

- **Best for**: Tech companies, startups, creative roles
- **Colors**: Bright blue accents, contemporary palette
- **Typography**: Modern sans-serif fonts
- **Layout**: Clean lines, plenty of white space

### 4. Executive

- **Best for**: Senior management, C-level positions
- **Colors**: Sophisticated black and gray tones
- **Typography**: Elegant, authoritative fonts
- **Layout**: Premium feel with refined spacing

### 5. Creative

- **Best for**: Design, marketing, creative industries
- **Colors**: Purple accents, artistic flair
- **Typography**: Stylish fonts with personality
- **Layout**: Unique sections, creative elements

### 6. Minimal

- **Best for**: Any industry, clean aesthetic preference
- **Colors**: Pure black and white, no distractions
- **Typography**: Simple, highly readable fonts
- **Layout**: Ultra-clean, content-focused

### 7. Corporate

- **Best for**: Finance, consulting, law firms
- **Colors**: Conservative grays, professional appearance
- **Typography**: Traditional business fonts
- **Layout**: Formal structure, traditional sections

### 8. Elegant

- **Best for**: Luxury brands, hospitality, premium services
- **Colors**: Warm browns, sophisticated earth tones
- **Typography**: Refined, elegant typefaces
- **Layout**: Sophisticated spacing and proportions

## Resume Layout Styles

### Chronological

- Work experience listed in reverse chronological order
- Best for: Consistent career progression
- Highlights: Career growth and stability

### Functional

- Skills and achievements grouped by category
- Best for: Career changers, gaps in employment
- Highlights: Relevant skills over timeline

### Combination

- Blend of chronological and functional formats
- Best for: Experienced professionals with diverse skills
- Highlights: Both skills and career progression

### Modern/Creative

- Non-traditional layouts with visual elements
- Best for: Creative industries, portfolio-based roles
- Highlights: Design skills and creativity

### ATS-Friendly

- Simple, clean format optimized for applicant tracking systems
- Best for: Large corporations, online applications
- Highlights: Keyword optimization and parseability

## Industry-Specific Recommendations

### Technology

- **Themes**: Modern, Professional, Minimal
- **Focus**: Technical skills, projects, certifications
- **Format**: Combination or functional

### Finance/Banking

- **Themes**: Corporate, Executive, Professional
- **Focus**: Quantifiable achievements, education
- **Format**: Chronological

### Creative Industries

- **Themes**: Creative, Modern, Elegant
- **Focus**: Portfolio links, creative projects
- **Format**: Modern/Creative

### Healthcare

- **Themes**: Professional, Classic, Corporate
- **Focus**: Certifications, clinical experience
- **Format**: Chronological

### Education

- **Themes**: Classic, Professional, Minimal
- **Focus**: Teaching experience, publications
- **Format**: Chronological or combination

### Sales/Marketing

- **Themes**: Modern, Creative, Professional
- **Focus**: Results, metrics, campaigns
- **Format**: Combination

## Typography Guidelines

### Professional Fonts

- **Headers**: Montserrat, Open Sans, Lato
- **Body**: Source Sans Pro, Roboto, Inter
- **Accent**: Playfair Display, Merriweather

### Font Sizes

- **Name**: 24-28px
- **Section Headers**: 16-18px
- **Job Titles**: 14-16px
- **Body Text**: 11-12px
- **Contact Info**: 10-11px

### Gray Tones

### Black & White

- Clean, modern, versatile

- Creative, innovative, unique

## Layout Best Practices

### Margins

- **Standard**: 0.5-1 inch on all sides
- **Tight**: 0.5 inch (for more content)
- **Generous**: 1 inch (for executive roles)

### Sections Order

1. Contact Information
2. Professional Summary/Objective
3. Core Skills/Technical Skills
4. Professional Experience
5. Education
6. Certifications
7. Additional Sections (Languages, Volunteer, etc.)

### Visual Hierarchy

- Use consistent spacing between sections
- Employ different font weights for emphasis
- Maintain alignment throughout
- Use bullet points for easy scanning
- Keep consistent date formatting

## Print Considerations

- Ensure themes work well in black and white
- Test print quality on standard paper
- Maintain readability at 100% and 85% scale
- Consider ink usage for colored themes
